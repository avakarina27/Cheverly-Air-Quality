Building the Network

Since 2018, the Cheverly community has worked with the University of Maryland (CEEJH) to build a hyper-local monitoring network. This was born from concerns regarding diesel traffic, concrete industries, and metal recycling plants.

Hyper-Local Monitoring

Unlike regional EPA stations, our ~30 sensors are located directly in neighborhoods. We use PurpleAir (PM2.5), C-12 (Black Carbon), and Quant-AQ (Gases) to get a full picture of street-level air quality.

The CE-AQI Methodology

The Cumulative Exposure Air Quality Index (CE-AQI) is a pilot effort to fix the "misleading" nature of standard reporting. Traditional AQI tells people the air is healthy because it ignores high-risk pollutants like Black Carbon.

The "Base + Adder" Formula

We calculate specific risks for each pollutant. We start with the highest score (Base) and then add 1% to 3% "adders" for every other pollutant present. This ensures we account for the total health burden of the "complex mixture" in our air.

Who Is Taking Action?

Cheverly is currently the leading community in Maryland for reporting potential air violations to the Maryland Department of the Environment (MDE). This partnership helps focus state inspectors on the most critical local problems.

See Something, Say Something

Report dust, smoke, or unusual smells to the MDE Hotline: (410) 537-3215. Your 5-minute report directly influences how state resources are assigned to our neighborhood.
