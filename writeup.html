Building the Hyper-Local Network

Since 2007, community members from Cheverly, Fairmount Heights, Seat Pleasant, and Cedar Heights have been concerned about health risks from heavy diesel traffic and neighboring industries (concrete batching, recycling, and warehouse distribution).

The Partners:

In 2018, the Center for Community Engagement, Environmental Justice, and Health (CEEJH) at UMD established the first PurpleAir sensors. In 2021, the Maryland Department of the Environment (MDE) began using this hyper-local data for the Cheverly Targeted Inspection Initiative.

The Equipment: Approximately 30 monitors, including PurpleAir (PM2.5), C-12 (Black Carbon), and Quant-AQ Modulair (Ozone, NOX, CO) sensors.

The CE-AQI Methodology: Why it Matters

Traditional AQIs ignore several high-risk pollutants. The EJAT feels strongly that continuing to tell people the air is "healthy" when cumulative risks are ignored is both misleading and irresponsible.

How we calculate the CE-AQI:

1. Pollutant Specifics: We calculate individual AQIs for PM2.5 (24-hour and annual) and Black Carbon (C-12).
2. Identify the Base: The pollutant with the highest AQI score becomes our "Base Value."
3. The Adders: Instead of ignoring other pollutants, we add "adders" (1% to 3% conservative increases) for each additional pollutant detected. This reflects the cumulative burden on the lungs.

Who Is Taking Action?

The community has established a bi-weekly workgroup with assistance from UMCP and MDE to review air monitoring data and industrial compliance.

See Something, Say Something

MDE has ~30 inspectors for 10,000 sources in Maryland. They need your help. If you see dust, smell odors, or see heavy smoke: Call the MDE Air Pollution Hotline: (410) 537-3215 or email mde.webmaster@maryland.gov.

Cheverly is currently the top reporter of potential air violations in the state, directly influencing where inspectors are sent.
