Understanding the CE-AQI Concept

The EJAT Network uses a unique "Cumulative Exposure - Air Quality Index" (CE-AQI) to communicate the health risks of multiple pollutants simultaneously. Cumulative exposure is real and significant, and we have a responsibility to communicate what all the data says.

Calculation Step 1: Pollutant Specific AQIs

We first calculate individual scores for every pollutant measured at a site:

PurpleAir Sensors
  • PM2.5: 24-hour AQI based on the 24-hour National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS).
  • Annual Average: A long-term AQI based on the previous year's average concentration.
Quant-AQ Modulair
  • Ozone: Highest daily rolling 8-hour average.
  • CO & NO2: Highest daily rolling 1-hour and 8-hour averages compared to NAAQS.
  • PM10: 24-hour AQI for fugitive dust monitoring.
Black Carbon / Diesel Particulate Matter (DPM)

Black Carbon measurements are converted to estimate DPM (80% ratio). We then compare these to both non-cancer (600 ng/M3) and cancer-based (33 ng/M3) benchmarks.

Calculation Step 2: The Cumulative Base & Adders

To determine the final CE-AQI, we identify the highest individual pollutant score from Step 1 as the Base Value. We then apply "Adders" to account for additional pollutants:

  • +1% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is between 80 and 99.
  • +2% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is between 100 and 119.
  • +3% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is 120 or higher.
