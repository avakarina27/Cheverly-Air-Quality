Community Insights: Using the CE-AQI Concept

Cumulative exposure is real and significant. While many experts are still working on how to address this issue, EJAT believes that saying nothing is irresponsible. [cite_start]We have a responsibility to communicate what all the data says[cite: 8, 9, 10].

Step 1: Calculate Pollutant Specific AQIs

[cite_start]

For every monitor or sensor in the Cheverly area, we first calculate individual scores based on the following hardware[cite: 13]:

PurpleAir Sensors

    [cite_start]
  • PM2.5: We calculate the 24-hour AQI using the 24-hour NAAQS[cite: 15].
    • [cite_start]
  • Long-term: We calculate an annual AQI using the average concentration over the previous year compared to the annual NAAQS[cite: 16, 17].

Quant-AQ Modulair

    [cite_start]
  • Ozone: Calculated using the highest daily rolling 8-hour average[cite: 21].
    • [cite_start]
  • CO & NO2: Calculated using the highest daily rolling 1-hour and 8-hour averages[cite: 29, 31, 34].
    • [cite_start]
  • Fugitive Dust: 24-hour AQI calculated for PM10[cite: 37, 38].

Black Carbon / Diesel Particulate Matter (DPM)

[cite_start]

We convert Black Carbon measurements to estimate DPM by multiplying by 80%[cite: 45].

    [cite_start]
  • Non-cancer: Calculated using a 600 ng/M3 benchmark[cite: 41].
    • [cite_start]
  • Cancer-based: Calculated using the 33 ng/M3 de minimis cancer risk benchmark established by the EPA[cite: 46].

Step 2: The Cumulative Exposure - AQI

[cite_start]

To provide a single health risk number, we identify the highest individual AQI value from Step 1 to serve as the "Base Value"[cite: 49, 51].

The Additive Logic: [cite_start]

If other pollutants are also elevated, we add the following "adders" to the Base Value[cite: 56]:

    [cite_start]
  • 1% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is between 80 and 99[cite: 63].
    • [cite_start]
  • 2% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is between 100 and 119[cite: 63].
    • [cite_start]
  • 3% Adder: If a secondary pollutant AQI is greater than 119[cite: 63].
