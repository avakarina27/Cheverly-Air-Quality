Understanding the CE-AQI Concept

The EJAT Network uses a "Cumulative Exposure - Air Quality Index" (CE-AQI) to communicate the health risks of multiple pollutants simultaneously. Cumulative exposure is real and significant, and we have a responsibility to communicate what the data says.

Pollutant Specific AQIs

Individual scores are calculated for every pollutant at a site using local hardware:

PurpleAir Sensors
  • PM2.5: Fine particulate matter from combustion, smoke, and dust.
  • Annual Average: Long-term exposure tracking for local health trends.
Quant-AQ Modulair
  • Ozone: Ground-level gas formed by sunlight and vehicle emissions.
  • CO & NO2: Primary indicators of heavy traffic and industrial exhaust.
  • Fugitive Dust: Larger particles from construction or industrial sites.
Diesel Particulate Matter (DPM)

Specific tracking of soot and toxic components released by diesel engines in high-traffic corridors.

Air Quality Categories

Category AQI Value Health Description
Good 0 to 50 Satisfactory; little or no risk.
Moderate 51 to 100 Acceptable; risk for unusually sensitive people.
Unhealthy (Sensitive) 101 to 150 Sensitive groups may experience health effects.
Unhealthy 151 to 200 Public may experience health effects.
Very Unhealthy 201 to 300 Increased health risk for everyone.
