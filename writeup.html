Understanding the CE-AQI Concept

[cite_start]

The EJAT Network uses a "Cumulative Exposure - Air Quality Index" (CE-AQI) to communicate the health risks of multiple pollutants simultaneously[cite: 240, 248]. [cite_start]Cumulative exposure is real and significant, and we have a responsibility to communicate what the data says[cite: 247, 248].

Step 1: Pollutant Specific AQIs

[cite_start]

Individual scores are calculated for every pollutant at a site using local hardware[cite: 250, 251]:

PurpleAir Sensors
    [cite_start]
  • PM2.5: 24-hour AQI calculated using the 24-hour NAAQS[cite: 253].
    • [cite_start]
  • Annual Average: Long-term AQI based on the previous year's average concentration[cite: 254, 255].
Quant-AQ Modulair
    [cite_start]
  • Ozone: 8-hour AQI using the highest daily rolling 8-hour average[cite: 258, 259].
    • [cite_start]
  • CO & NO2: Highest daily rolling averages compared to federal standards[cite: 266, 267, 271, 272].
    • [cite_start]
  • Fugitive Dust: 24-hour AQI for PM10[cite: 275, 276].
Diesel Particulate Matter (DPM) [cite_start]

Black Carbon measurements are converted to estimate DPM by a factor of 80%[cite: 283]. [cite_start]We calculate both non-cancer (600 ng/M3 benchmark) and cancer-based (33 ng/M3 benchmark) risk levels[cite: 279, 280, 284].

Step 2: Cumulative Adders

[cite_start]

The highest individual pollutant score serves as the Base Value[cite: 287, 289]. [cite_start]If other pollutants are also elevated, we apply small "Adders" to reflect the total health impact[cite: 294, 301, 309]:

    [cite_start]
  • +1% Adder: For secondary pollutant AQIs between 80 and 99[cite: 301].
    • [cite_start]
  • +2% Adder: For secondary pollutant AQIs between 100 and 119[cite: 301].
    • [cite_start]
  • +3% Adder: For secondary pollutant AQIs of 120 or higher[cite: 301].
